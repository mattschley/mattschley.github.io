COVID-19 Spread in America

Cases and Deaths Measured at the Beginning of the Month
April 1st, 2020

By April 1st, 2020, COVID-19 is detected in all 50 states (this actually occured a few weeks earlier, in mid-March), showing just how fast the pandemic had spread across the country. This was also a few weeks after most major cities had implemented stay at home orders, so this data essentially shows the result of several months of virus spreading without any major shutdowns being enacted by the federal government.



Of special note is the massive number of cases and deaths reported in New York City. They were the most heavily impacted city in America, and the beginning of April was characterized by massive hospitalization, PPE shortages, and a call for emergency workers to help deal with the growing pandemic.
